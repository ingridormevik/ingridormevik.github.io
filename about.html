About

I study Digital Culture at the University of Bergen. Second year. This semester it is DIKULT216 Digital Games and an internship at the Centre for Digital Narrative, where I am part of the SurvivingSOGICE research group.

This is where I put things. Essays, reflections, experiments. The thread running through all of it is the same: what does technology actually do to people, and who gets to decide that.

Research at CDN

I am interning on the SurvivingSOGICE project at CDN. We document Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity Change Efforts across Europe. Conversion therapy. My part is database work in Zotero, mapping organisations, outreach work and building a interactive work for the project website where survivors can share their stories safely.

Activism

I run social media and content strategy for Bergen Pride. I have been doing that since 2023. One thing I found during my DIKULT106 research: Norwegian Pride content reached 273,000 people. English content got suppressed. The algorithm does not treat all queerness equally. I wrote about that as strategic invisibility.

I am on the board of the Western DepartmentFRI Vestland and run their social media. I started KIM as a collective for women, non-binary and trans people in Bergen. It became a FRI subgroup in late 2025. Last Christmas I organised Skeiv Jul, seven events across December for people who would otherwise spend the holidays alone. About 40 people came to the Christmas Eve dinner. We got 75,000 kr from Vestland Fylkeskommune for it.

Regnbuenatt 2026

Right now I am building Regnbuenatt 2026, the closing party for Bergen Pride. It is a Twine game and a real event at Marinehagen with DJs and performers. The concept uses Norwegian folklore creatures as queer archetypes: Huldra, Nøkken, Bergtrollet, Fossegrimen, Draugen. Each one maps onto something. When I was building it I discovered that Android lets you vibrate the phone from HTML but Apple locks that completely. So on iOS you get a low-frequency audio tone through headphones instead, bone conduction. That ended up becoming part of the essay about the whole thing.
